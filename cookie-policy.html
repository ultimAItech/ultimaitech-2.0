Cookie Policy

Last Updated: December 10, 2024

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide a better user experience by remembering your preferences and understanding how you use our services.

2. How We Use Cookies

ultimAItech uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Functions: Authentication, security, and basic site functionality
  • Performance: Analytics and site optimization
  • Personalization: Language preferences and UI customization
  • Security: Fraud prevention and protection against attacks

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for our website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as:

  • Session Management: Maintaining your logged-in state
  • Security: CSRF protection and authentication
  • Load Balancing: Routing requests to appropriate servers

Examples:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
session_id User session management Session
csrf_token Security protection Session
auth_token Authentication 7 days

3.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information:

  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Navigation paths and click patterns
  • Error messages and technical issues
  • Device and browser information

Examples:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
_ga Google Analytics user ID 2 years
_gid Google Analytics session ID 24 hours
analytics_session Internal analytics tracking 30 days

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable personalized features and remember your preferences:

  • Language Selection: Remember your preferred language
  • UI Preferences: Dark mode, layout settings
  • Form Data: Remember previously entered information

Examples:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
lang_pref Language preference 1 year
ui_theme UI theme preference 1 year
cookie_consent Cookie consent choices 1 year

3.4 Targeting/Advertising Cookies

We do not currently use advertising cookies. If we introduce them in the future, we will:

  • Obtain your explicit consent
  • Provide clear opt-out options
  • Update this policy accordingly

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third-party services we use:

4.1 Google Analytics

  • Purpose: Website traffic analysis
  • Provider: Google LLC
  • Privacy Policy: Google Privacy Policy
  • Opt-out: Google Analytics Opt-out

4.2 Content Delivery Networks (CDN)

  • Purpose: Fast and reliable content delivery
  • Cookies: Load balancing and performance optimization
  • Duration: Session-based

5. How to Control Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies or specific cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Clear cookies when closing the browser

Browser-Specific Instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Cookie Consent Tool

When you first visit our website, you can choose which cookies to accept:

  • Accept All: Allows all cookies for full functionality
  • Necessary Only: Only essential cookies (may limit features)
  • Customize: Choose specific cookie categories

You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our footer.

5.3 Impact of Blocking Cookies

If you block or delete cookies, some features may not work properly:

  • You may need to log in repeatedly
  • Preferences won't be saved
  • Some interactive features may be unavailable
  • Website performance may be affected

6. Other Tracking Technologies

6.1 Local Storage

We use browser local storage to:

  • Cache application data for faster loading
  • Store user preferences locally
  • Enable offline functionality

6.2 Session Storage

We use session storage for:

  • Temporary data during your visit
  • Form state preservation
  • Navigation state management

6.3 Web Beacons/Pixels

We may use web beacons to:

  • Track email opens and clicks
  • Measure campaign effectiveness
  • Understand user engagement

7. Mobile Applications

If you use our mobile applications, we may collect:

  • Device Identifiers: Unique device IDs
  • Location Data: With your permission
  • Usage Analytics: App interaction data
  • Crash Reports: Technical error information

You can manage these permissions in your device settings.

8. Do Not Track (DNT)

Our website currently does not respond to Do Not Track (DNT) signals because there is no industry standard for how to interpret them. We respect your privacy choices made through our cookie consent tool and browser settings.

9. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect:

  • Changes in cookie usage or technologies
  • Legal or regulatory requirements
  • New features or services

Material changes will be communicated via:

  • Prominent notice on our website
  • Email notification to registered users
  • Updated "Last Updated" date at the top
